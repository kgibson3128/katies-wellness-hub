Daily Planning Method
📋 Daily Planning — 9:15am (15 mins)
1
Check the landscape
Open the shared timetable — what are the big events today? Any trips, special activities, or unusual schedule changes?
2
Check your notes & parking lot
Student birthdays or special occasions. Unresolved issues from yesterday. Review your parking lot Google Doc.
3
Build your Todoist list
Prioritised order — not by time, just by importance. Ask yourself:
  • What must happen today?
  • What would be great to get done?
  • What can wait if needed?
Use pen and paper to think it through first if helpful. Use priority flags for anything that truly can't slip.
4
Sense check
Any interruptions likely today — Zoom call, busy handover, known student issues? Anything to delegate to a supervisor?
Close-Down Method
📋 Close-Down — 3:30pm (30 mins)
1
Parking lot
Anything unresolved or unfinished goes into your Google Doc parking lot. Write down exactly where you got to so tomorrow-you doesn't have to remember.
2
Tomorrow's morning meeting prep
Check tomorrow's shared timetable and brief doc. Note the key things to communicate — evening activity, student birthdays, delegations, reminders. Add any context you'll need to explain in person. Well-briefed supervisors = protected evenings.
3
Clear your head
Review Todoist — anything left undone that needs flagging? Any issues to hand off before you finish? Write down anything that's nagging at you.
4
Close down ✅
Close unnecessary tabs and apps. You're done with focused work for the day.
Productivity Toolkit
The One Task Rule
Before you open anything, decide what your current task is. When you notice yourself drifting to another task, gently return. Add other tasks to Todoist so you don't lose them — but don't switch to them mid-flow.
Phone & Social Boundaries
  • During focused blocks — phone face down or on do not disturb
  • Headphones on = focus mode. Let this signal to others too
  • If you're in a shared space and can't fully focus, use the time for lighter reactive tasks rather than fighting for a flow state that won't come
The Two Minute Rule
If something unexpected lands and takes less than two minutes — deal with it immediately. If it takes longer, add it to Todoist and return to your current task.
Unexpected Task Filter
When something urgent lands, ask yourself:
  • Will something bad happen in the next hour if I don't deal with this? → Deal with it now
  • Is this actually someone else's job? → Delegate it
  • Can this wait until my reactive block? → Add to Todoist and park it
  • Is this going to keep nagging at me? → Add to parking lot and consciously set it aside
Flow State Protection
Morning 9:15–12:00 is your best focus time — protect it fiercely. Save emails, messages, and reactive tasks for the afternoon block where possible. If you're in a good flow state, don't stop for anything non-urgent.

ONE CONSCIOUS TREAT PER WEEK
Something chosen in advance — a great coffee, something tasty, a cold diet coke. Planned, not reactive. Enjoyed fully and guilt-free. This is different from emotional eating.
Nutrition Advice
Plate Structure — Every Dining Hall Meal
½ plate vegetables or salad  ·  ¼ plate protein  ·  ¼ plate carbs

Always start with salad or veg before reaching for the main. One plate, no seconds — unless it's salad. Sit down and eat properly.
Your Personal Stash
Keep in your room: protein yoghurts, fruit, pre-cut veg sticks, nuts or nut butter sachets, herbal teas. Use as breakfast top-ups, afternoon snacks, or post-gym fuel.
Water
Full glass on waking before coffee. Large bottle with you all day. 2–2.5 litres target. If you feel like snacking, drink water first and wait 10 minutes.
Emotional Eating Game Plan
  • Pause rule: Am I actually hungry, or stressed/tired/frustrated? Wait 5 minutes.
  • Redirect: Use the emotional eating menu above.
  • Permission slip: If you do emotionally eat, do not spiral. Reset at the next meal, not tomorrow.

🌿 Step outside for 5–10 minutes during the day — even on the busiest days.
🚨 Emergency Reset Protocol
When a truly bad week hits and you've fallen off everything.
Don't try to catch up all at once
Pick one thing to restart. Just one.
Start with the evening routine
It's the easiest thing you control and it signals safety to your nervous system. Shower, skincare, bed. That's enough.
Get outside & drink water
Even 10 minutes of fresh air changes your state. Then a big glass of water. Small things.
Reframe what success looks like
Maintaining 60% of a good plan during a crisis is a win, not a failure. You are doing brilliantly.
